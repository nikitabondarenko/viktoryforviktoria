Summary

This is a plea for help for an immigrant woman who spent 17 years building two thriving ballet companies -- one nonprofit, one for-profit. The Board of the nonprofit, acting illegally and with personal motivations, wrongfully fired the founder from all her positions.

Her story needs to be heard. Read it. Share it. Speak out. And if you can, consider making a small contribution to help cover her legal costs.

The Story

The Beginning (2007-2019)

Ms. Viktoria Titova, a former Bolshoi Ballet ballerina, founded Emerald Ballet Theatre (EBT), a nonprofit organization, in 2007 and Emerald Ballet Academy (EBA), a for-profit ballet school, in 2010. Viktoria single-handedly built EBT and EBA from ground up, recruiting talented teachers and staff. The two entities complemented each other perfectly — one focused on nurturing students, while the other brought their talents to life on stage through stunning performances.

She pursuit this dream with immense financial and personal sacrifice, working tirelessly -- without vacations or proper compensation -- to provide the Seattle arts community with the highest level of ballet education.

The Beginning of Changes (2019)

In 2019, one of Viktoria's teachers, Erin McEachran, whom she had hired and mentored for years, suggested adding her father, James McEachran, to the EBT Board. This would prove to be a fateful decision. Five years later, James orchestrated Viktoria's ousting—paving the way for his daughter to take her place.

Troubling Signs (Spring 2024)

  • Record enrollment numbers
  • Prestigious competition awards
  • Successful performance season
  • $300,000 surplus in EBT's bank account
  • Yet the Board began fabricating concerns about leadership
  • Several employees engaged in acts of sabotage

The Escalation

Language Barrier Issues

  • Viktoria requested an interpreter for Board meetings
  • Request denied three times
  • Only provided after attorney intervention
  • Left without interpreter during critical May meetings

The Illegal Termination (June 4-5, 2024)

The Board's Actions:

  • Marathon meeting spanning two days
  • Pretended to work toward resolution for hours
  • Suddenly presented pre-written motion to remove Viktoria
  • Removed from all positions without proper notice
  • Violated Washington State law (RCW Section 24.03a.530(2)(b))
  • Clear conflicts of interest ignored

The Aftermath

Impact on EBT:

  • Only 10% of students remained
  • First time in 17 years without proper Nutcracker performance
  • Community divided

Impact on Viktoria:

  • Lost studio space
  • Lost costumes and sets
  • Faced overwhelming legal costs

Viktoria's Resilience

December 2024:

  • Secured new studio space
  • Staged four spectacular Nutcracker performances
  • Featured live orchestra and stunning new sets

January 2025:

  • EBA students dominated Youth America Grand Prix (YAGP)
  • Earned "Outstanding Teacher" Award (given to only two schools)
  • Most talented teachers remained with Viktoria
  • Majority of students chose to stay with EBA
